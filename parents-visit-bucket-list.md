# 100 Bucket List Ideas — Bavarian Cities Within ~1.5h of Munich

A mix of indoor, outdoor, food, drink, quirky, scenic, and cultural. Pick names that match Google Places search.

## Nuremberg (Nürnberg)

1. **Kaiserburg Nürnberg** — Imperial castle on the sandstone ridge with views over the rooftops. Indoor halls and outdoor ramparts.
2. **Henkersteg (Hangman's Bridge)** — Covered medieval wooden bridge that was the city executioner's quarters.
3. **Lebkuchen-Schmidt Lebkuchenhaus am Plobenhofstraße** — Flagship gingerbread shop with tasting counter and historic packaging.
4. **Albrecht-Dürer-Haus** — The painter's actual home, restored, with a working historic print shop.
5. **Handwerkerhof** — Walled craftsmen's courtyard by the Königstor, with leatherworkers, glassblowers, and a tiny bratwurst stand.
6. **Bratwursthäusle bei St. Sebald** — Bratwurst grilled over beech wood since 1312.
7. **Zum Gulden Stern** — Possibly the oldest bratwurst kitchen in the world (1419).
8. **Barfüßer Hausbrauerei** — Brewery in a vaulted medieval granary cellar with copper kettles in the dining room.
9. **Im Keller (The Vault)** — Dinner in an 800-year-old stone vault.
10. **Lorenzkirche** — Towering Gothic interior with Veit Stoss's hanging "Angelic Salutation."
11. **Sebalduskirche** — The other major Gothic church, with the bronze Sebaldus tomb.
12. **Schöner Brunnen on Hauptmarkt** — Gilded fountain; spin the golden ring for luck.
13. **Tiergarten Nürnberg** — Rocky-landscape zoo with dolphin lagoon and manatees, partly covered.
14. **Bergbrand Kaffeerösterei** — Specialty roaster on a cobbled corner near the castle.
15. **Café Reichold** — Classic Franconian Konditorei for Streuselkuchen and coffee.
16. **Felsengänge (Historical Rock-Cut Cellars)** — Skip if you're avoiding underground; otherwise a quirky guided beer-history tour. *(flag: subterranean)*
17. **Tucher Bräu Mälzerei viewing tour** — Working maltings near the river.
18. **Christkindlesmarkt walking route** (off season) — Trace where the world's most famous Christmas market sets up.
19. **Kettensteg (Chain Bridge)** — Oldest surviving iron chain footbridge in continental Europe.
20. **Lady Nightwatchman tour** — Evening lantern walk in period costume.

## Regensburg

21. **Steinerne Brücke (Stone Bridge)** — 12th-century arched bridge across the Danube, walking icon of the city.
22. **Historische Wurstkuchl** — Sausage shack by the bridge, serving since the 1100s.
23. **Regensburger Dom (St. Peter's Cathedral)** — Gothic cathedral with the world's largest "swallow's nest" organ.
24. **Domspatzen vespers** — Hear the world's oldest boys' choir if timing aligns.
25. **Goldener Turm climb** — One of the surviving Italian-style "family towers" you can ascend.
26. **Altes Rathaus & Reichstagsmuseum** — Tour the original Imperial Diet hall and an actual medieval interrogation chamber.
27. **Spitalgarten beer garden** — Across the Danube, chestnut shade, brewery on site.
28. **Kneitinger Stammhaus** — Old-school Bavarian beer hall, dark Bock.
29. **Café Felix** — Three-level café/bar institution with a leafy courtyard.
30. **Bischofshof am Dom** — Beer garden in the courtyard of the bishop's brewery.
31. **Dreieinigkeitskirche tower** — Climb for rooftop panoramas and "lace-like" stucco ceiling inside.
32. **Schloss Thurn und Taxis** — Working royal palace with state rooms and a quiet cloister.
33. **Goliathhaus mural** — 16th-century giant David vs Goliath fresco on a residential façade.
34. **Schifffahrt Klinger boat trip to Walhalla** — Danube cruise to Ludwig I's neoclassical hall of fame above the river.
35. **Walhalla Memorial** — Marble Greek-temple-style hall packed with German busts.
36. **Weltenburger am Dom** — Pretzel-crusted Bockbierschnitzel.
37. **Café Orphee** — Belle-époque French bistro on a side street, hidden hotel rooms upstairs.
38. **Hutmuseum (Hat Museum) — Hutgalerie Maria Niedermeier** — Tiny private hat collection by appointment.
39. **Princess Café (Café Prinzess)** — One of Germany's oldest coffee houses (1686).

## Augsburg

40. **Fuggerei** — 1521 walled almshouse village; residents still pay one guilder/year.
41. **Augsburger Puppenkiste** — Legendary marionette theatre; book a show ahead.
42. **Goldener Saal im Rathaus** — 14m gilt ceiling banqueting hall.
43. **Augsburger Dom** — Romanesque/Gothic cathedral with the oldest figurative stained glass in the world (Prophets' Windows).
44. **Schaezlerpalais & Rococo ballroom** — Plus Veronese, Dürer, Tiepolo paintings.
45. **Maximilianstraße stroll** — Wide Renaissance boulevard with three monumental fountains (Augustus, Mercury, Hercules).
46. **St. Anna-Kirche & Luther's rooms** — Where Luther was hauled before Cardinal Cajetan in 1518.
47. **Brechthaus** — Birthplace of Bertolt Brecht, intimate biographical museum.
48. **Mozarthaus Augsburg** — Birthplace of Mozart's father Leopold.
49. **Augsburger Wasserwerk am Roten Tor** — UNESCO water-management system; canals, towers, historic pump station.
50. **Riegele Wirtshaus** — Family brewery taphouse opposite the train station with a sampler flight.
51. **Augsburger Marionetten-bistro Die Kresse** — Tiny restaurant; reserve early.
52. **Stadtmarkt Augsburg** — Lively covered market hall for lunch crawl.
53. **Perlachturm climb** — Renaissance bell tower next to the Rathaus.

## Landshut

54. **Burg Trausnitz** — Hilltop residence with the "Narrentreppe" (Fool's Staircase) of life-size commedia dell'arte frescoes.
55. **St. Martinskirche** — Tallest brick church tower in the world (130m); climbable.
56. **Altstadt promenade** — Pastel arcaded main street between Heiliggeist and Martinskirche.
57. **Neue Burgschänke at Trausnitz** — Lunch with panorama over the old town.
58. **Stadtresidenz Landshut** — First Italian Renaissance palace north of the Alps, in the middle of the high street.
59. **KOENIGmuseum** — Sculptor Fritz Koenig's bronze beasts (he made the WTC plaza sphere).
60. **Hofreitschule courtyard café** — Quiet espresso stop in a former riding school.
61. **Ländtor** — Last surviving city gate; arch through into the old town.
62. **Hofgarten Landshut** — Castle gardens — outdoor when the rain lifts.
63. **Café Wunderbar** — Bookshop café local favorite.

## Bamberg

64. **Altes Rathaus auf der Regnitz** — Painted town hall on an island; cross both bridges to see all sides.
65. **Klein Venedig** — Half-timbered former fishermen's quarter along the Regnitz.
66. **Bamberger Dom** — Cathedral with the mysterious anonymous "Bamberg Horseman" sculpture (~1230).
67. **Diözesanmuseum** — Houses the Star Mantle of Emperor Henry II (1020s, golden embroidered cosmology).
68. **Neue Residenz & Rose Garden** — Baroque palace with hilltop garden views.
69. **Schlenkerla (Brauerei Heller-Trum)** — Pour a Rauchbier in the original 1400s brewery.
70. **Brauerei Spezial** — The other rauchbier house; more subtle smoke.
71. **Ambräusianum** — Tiny city-center brewpub with a tasting flight.
72. **Spezial-Keller terrace** — Brewery garden looking down over the cathedral.
73. **Kloster Michaelsberg ceiling** — Painted vault of 600+ herbs, flowers, and animals.
74. **Konditorei Café Müller** — Bamberg Hörnla pastries.
75. **E.T.A. Hoffmann Haus** — Tiny home of the writer who invented the Nutcracker tale.
76. **Bootsfahrt mit der Personenschifffahrt Kropf** — Regnitz boat trip past Klein Venedig.
77. **Weyermann Mälzerei** — Maltster behind the world's smoked-malt supply; tours by booking.

## Würzburg

78. **Würzburger Residenz** — UNESCO baroque palace with Tiepolo's enormous staircase fresco.
79. **Hofgarten der Residenz** — Free, formal palace gardens (outdoor option).
80. **Staatlicher Hofkeller wine tasting** — Tasting in the historic palace cellars.
81. **Festung Marienberg** — Fortress on the vine-covered hill over the city; walk up across the Old Main Bridge.
82. **Alte Mainbrücke "Brückenschoppen"** — Drink a glass of Franconian Silvaner standing on the bridge.
83. **Würzburger Dom St. Kilian** — Romanesque cathedral with a sharply modern reinterpreted choir.
84. **Neumünster** — Baroque dome next door, on the burial site of St. Kilian.
85. **Käppele** — Pilgrimage church on the hill opposite the fortress, with a Stations-of-the-Cross route up.
86. **Juliusspital Weingut** — One of Germany's oldest wine estates inside a working baroque hospital.
87. **Bürgerspital Weinstube** — Sister wine estate with rustic Stube and Bocksbeutel flights.
88. **Marktplatz & Falkenhaus** — Rococo curlicue façade beside the gothic Marienkapelle.
89. **Backöfele restaurant** — Warren of low-ceilinged Franconian dining rooms in an old courtyard.
90. **Weingut am Stein hilltop terrace** — Modern winery above town with floor-to-ceiling glass tasting room.

## Eichstätt & Altmühltal

91. **Willibaldsburg** — Hilltop fortress with the Jura-Museum's archaeopteryx fossil (a real one).
92. **Eichstätt Dom & Mortuarium** — Strange double-cathedral with a memorial cloister of carved tombstones.
93. **Hofgarten Eichstätt** — Baroque gardens with the unusual round Schlossgarten temple.
94. **Hofmühl Brauereigaststätte** — Local brewery restaurant for unfiltered lagers.
95. **Steinbruch Blumenberg fossil quarry** — Hammer your own Jurassic fossils; covered work pavilion.
96. **Steinbruch Mühlheim fossil quarry** — Smaller, quieter alternative for fossil hunting.
97. **Burg Prunn** — Cliff-edge castle above the Altmühl with the medieval Nibelungenlied manuscript story.
98. **Kloster Weltenburg on the Donaudurchbruch** — Baroque abbey at the Danube gorge; arrive by boat from Kelheim.
99. **Befreiungshalle Kelheim** — Ludwig I's circular victory hall above the river, all marble and gilt.
100. **Tropfsteinhöhle Schulerloch (or skip)** — Dripstone show cave near Essing. *(flag: subterranean — skip if your parents won't go underground)*

---

## Notes for adding to Lark

- Items 16, 100 are flagged as underground/cave — drop these if you're being strict.
- Most are walking distance from each city's main train station; Würzburg and Bamberg are at the edge of your 1h30 radius and probably warrant overnight if weather is grim.
- "Brückenschoppen" (#82) is the Würzburg tradition of drinking wine on the bridge — a genuinely lovely ritual even in light rain (umbrellas optional).
- The fossil quarries (#95, #96) typically operate spring-autumn; check opening before driving out.
